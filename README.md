## What Actually Makes Make Different

There are plenty of automation tools out there. Zapier is the most famous one. But here's the thing people figure out pretty quickly: Zapier is great for simple trigger-action stuff, but the moment your workflow needs any real logic — conditional branching, looping through arrays, transforming data — it either can't do it or charges you a fortune.

Make handles all of that visually. You can see exactly how your data flows from one step to the next. Routers let you split into multiple paths based on conditions. Iterators let you process lists item by item. Error handlers let you build automations that actually recover gracefully instead of just dying silently at 3am.

And it connects to over 3,000+ apps — Google Sheets, Slack, HubSpot, Airtable, Salesforce, OpenAI, Anthropic's Claude API, and basically everything else you're probably already using.

## Plan-by-Plan: What You're Actually Getting

**Free** — 1,000 credits per month, 2 active scenarios, 15-minute minimum interval between runs. Solid for experimenting or running one or two lightweight automations. No credit card required to sign up. Genuinely free, forever.

**Core** — This is where things get real. Unlimited active scenarios, runs down to 1-minute intervals, access to the Make API (so you can trigger scenarios programmatically), webhooks, and higher data transfer limits. At around $10.59/month on annual billing, it's hard to argue with.

**Pro** — Everything in Core, plus priority scenario execution (your workflows jump the queue during peak hours), custom variables, and full-text execution log search. The priority execution is the big one — if you're running anything time-sensitive, like a workflow that needs to respond within seconds, Pro is worth the extra few dollars.

**Teams** — Built for collaboration. Adds team roles and permissions, shared scenario templates, higher API rate limits (240 calls/minute vs 120 on Pro), and live chat support. If you're an agency managing automations for clients or a team where multiple people need to get into the same workflows, this is your tier.

**Enterprise** — Custom credit volumes, SSO/SCIM, audit logs, enterprise app integrations, overage protection, and 24/7 support. For large orgs with compliance requirements or serious security standards.

If you need more credits than your plan includes, you can buy extra packs: **$9 for 10,000 additional operations**. Just note that auto-purchased overages include a 25% surcharge, so if you're consistently going over, upgrading your plan usually works out cheaper than buying extras.

## A Few Things Worth Knowing Before You Sign Up

The credit model is transparent, but it does require a little planning. A scenario that polls an app every minute runs 1,440 times a day — even if nothing happens. That burns credits fast. Use webhooks instead of polling whenever the app supports it, and you can dramatically cut your usage.

Credits don't expire, which is genuinely useful for seasonal or irregular workloads. And when you hit your monthly limit, scenarios pause rather than racking up surprise charges — you get email warnings at 80% and 100%.

Annual billing gives you the ~15% discount, and the Free plan is a solid place to start without any commitment. There's no time limit on the free tier.
